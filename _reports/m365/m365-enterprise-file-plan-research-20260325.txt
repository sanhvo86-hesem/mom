Applied design principles
- Official records are classified by function and record series, not by person name or desktop location.
- Employee-specific working material is separated into named workbenches so collaboration can happen without turning the official file plan into personal silos.
- Restricted personal records are split into a dedicated people site and shared restricted operations, rather than overusing folder-level permission breaks in general department areas.
- Digital administration records are split into 00-Current and {YYYY} so living controls and frozen history do not get mixed.
- Controlled source is separated from runtime/admin evidence, and mirrored from SharePoint local sync into Git and then server deployment, with reverse sync recorded back.
- Current data, active projects, year-based history, drafts, references, and archive are separated structurally to keep the template durable over many years.

Structural conclusions adopted in the template
- 3 site roots: HESEM-QMS-Core, HESEM-People-Restricted, HESEM-Digital-Control.
- Every employee has up to 3 storage loci: official record destination, named employee workbench, restricted people dossier when required.
- Every department has team workbenches plus named employee workbenches under role roots.
- People records include active employees, pending starters, former employees, contractor populations, visitors/vendor access, and restricted shared operations.
- Digital system records include both 00-Current and {YYYY} in every system area.
- Source control mirrors approved repo roots and excludes runtime payload, secrets, and transaction data.
- Job dossiers are customer-scoped and follow the gate structure G0 to G7 plus NCR/CAPA and archive lock.
