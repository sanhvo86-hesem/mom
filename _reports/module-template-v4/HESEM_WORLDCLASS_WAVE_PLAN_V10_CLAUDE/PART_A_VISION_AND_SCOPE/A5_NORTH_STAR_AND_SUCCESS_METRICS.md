# A5 — North Star & Success Metrics

This chapter defines what success looks like for HESEM. The North Star is
the directional statement of where HESEM is going. The success metrics are
the falsifiable measurements that say whether HESEM is on track. Without
explicit success metrics, "world-class" is opinion; with them, it is
verifiable.

---

## 1. The North Star

HESEM's North Star is:

> An Operations Operating Platform on which every operational fact about
> the manufacturing enterprise is generated by an authoritative root,
> recorded in the Operational Truth Graph, governed by a state machine,
> substantiated by evidence, gated by policy, traceable to a workflow
> event, anchored to an immutable audit chain, accountable to a named
> owner, and measurable against a published service-level objective.

If any of these nine conditions is missing for a fact in production, HESEM
is incomplete. The North Star is not a slogan; it is a contract with the
customer and with HESEM's own engineering discipline.

---

## 2. The four horizons

HESEM's progress toward the North Star is divided into four horizons:

### Horizon 0 (H0) — Safety + Inert (months 0 to 3 of the program)

**Goal**: do no harm to the current portal. HESEM Module Template V4 (HMV4)
prototype is fully feature-flagged inert by default. Cross-browser baseline
GREEN on chromium. No forbidden file modifications. Phase 2 integration
issues resolved or explicitly contained.

**What "done" looks like**:
- HMV4_PREVIEW_ENABLED = false on main branch
- Fixture file 74 not loaded by portal HTML
- Forbidden file diff over 90 days = 0 against baseline
- Cross-browser chromium baseline tests GREEN
- Wave 0 stabilization report PASS

### Horizon 1 (H1) — Pre-production readiness (months 3 to 12)

**Goal**: 18 Wave-1 roots reach maturity level 3 to 4 (current-portal-safe
E2E prototype to opt-in live read-only API). eQMS core slices
end-to-end tested. Operational Truth Graph nodes and edges populated.
Audit chain anchor cron operational.

**What "done" looks like**:
- 18 Wave-1 roots at L≥3
- Selected eQMS roots at L4 with live read-only graduated per ADR
- OTG node count > 100,000 (synthetic + live)
- Audit chain anchor cron 100% PASS over 30 days
- Per-route p95 latency ≤ 500 ms
- Per-route error rate ≤ 0.5%
- Accessibility serious violations = 0

### Horizon 2 (H2) — Controlled mutation (months 12 to 24)

**Goal**: Stage 3 mutation graduation across at least 10 transition
surfaces. Saga compensation tested. Audit chain integrity verified
under chaos.

**What "done" looks like**:
- ≥ 10 transitions at L5 with per-mutation ADR
- Audit chain integrity 100% PASS axiom A3 and A6
- Chaos tests for saga compensation 100% PASS over 1000-trial sample
- Workflow commit p95 ≤ 500 ms
- Rollback rehearsal evidence freshness ≤ 30 days
- Idempotency replay accuracy = 1.00

### Horizon 3 (H3) — Validation + productization (months 24 to 36)

**Goal**: First vertical pack at L7 productized. First pilot tenant in
pre-production. SOC 2 Type II audit prepared.

**What "done" looks like**:
- One vertical pack (Pharma OR Auto OR Aero) at L7
- ≥ 1 pilot tenant in pre-production
- Validation Master Plan + IQ / OQ / PQ executed
- SOC 2 Type II observation window started
- DORA Elite tier on ≥ 60% of services
- Audit pack export time ≤ 24 hours
- Pilot tenant CSAT ≥ 0.85

## 4. The probability honest assessment

V5 file 19 §9 estimated the cumulative probability of full plan delivery
within 3x the planned timeline at approximately 10%. This is sober and
acknowledged. HESEM does not promise deterministic delivery; HESEM promises
falsifiable progress. Quarterly re-baselining is mandatory. Per V3 RULE-5,
no wave starts without the predecessor wave's PASS.

The honest reading of the timeline:

```
Optimistic (full team, no surprises):           12 to 18 months to W12
Expected (full team, normal delivery):           18 to 24 months to W12
Pessimistic (lean team or major surprise):      24 to 36 months to W12
Solo Codex-augmented (founder + AI, contractors): 24 to 32 months to W12
```

HESEM's success is not measured by hitting the optimistic timeline. It is
measured by:

- Producing falsifiable progress at each gate (decision phrase emitted).
- Never compromising the discipline (RULE-1 to RULE-8 always honored).
- Never producing production wording before validation evidence.
- Producing real customer outcomes when customers begin pilots (Wave 11).

## 5. The stop signals

HESEM has explicit stop signals. If any of these is observed, the program
halts (not merely the current wave) and re-evaluates:

```
STOP-1  An audit chain hash break is detected in production.
         (Rationale: customer trust is broken; cannot resume until proven.)
STOP-2  An AI advisory commits one of the eight banned regulated decisions.
         (Rationale: regulatory boundary breach; halt AI and review.)
STOP-3  Tenant data leaks across tenant boundary in production.
         (Rationale: catastrophic; halt and notify customers.)
STOP-4  WORM evidence is lost (S3 Object Lock failure or equivalent).
         (Rationale: regulated retention guarantee broken.)
STOP-5  Cross-region DR drill fails 2 consecutive quarters.
         (Rationale: HA promise unmet; halt new tenant onboarding.)
STOP-6  SOC 2 Type II audit fails materially.
         (Rationale: compliance promise broken; halt sales.)
STOP-7  OTG axiom A1, A3, A5, A7, or A14 violated in production data.
         (Rationale: structural integrity broken; halt mutations.)
STOP-8  Cryptographic algorithm in audit chain becomes deprecated without
        successor migration plan.
         (Rationale: long-term audit integrity at risk.)
```

These eight stop signals are the absolute boundaries. The program
prioritizes their absence above any wave milestone.

## 6. Customer success outcomes (the metrics that ultimately count)

HESEM's success is ultimately measured by what customers experience:

```
- Cost of poor quality reduced 20-40% within 18 months of pre-production cutover.
- Audit pack export time reduced from typical "weeks of manual gathering"
  to under 24 hours.
- Implementation time for mid-market customer reduced from typical 18-36
  months for ERP+MES+QMS replacement to 4-8 months for HESEM Core/Pro tier.
- Net Revenue Retention >= 110% (Pro), >= 115% (Enterprise) annually.
- Customer Logo Retention >= 95% per year.
- Customer Net Promoter Score >= 50 across the customer base.
- Average customer reference willingness >= 80% of customers willing to
  speak to peers about HESEM after one year.
```

These customer outcomes are the long-form definition of "world-class."
