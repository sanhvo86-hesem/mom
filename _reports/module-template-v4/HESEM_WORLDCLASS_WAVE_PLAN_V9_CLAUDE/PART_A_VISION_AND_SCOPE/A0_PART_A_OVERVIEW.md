# PART_A — VISION & SCOPE — Overview

Part A answers four questions before any architectural or capability planning
begins:

```
A1.  WHAT is HESEM?               (the product vision and the problem it solves)
A2.  WHO is it for?                (target customers, stakeholder map, decision makers)
A3.  WHAT is in scope?             (the 14 domains and the 95 roots)
A4.  WHAT discipline frames it?    (standards, regulatory landscape, pre-production posture)
A5.  WHAT does success look like?  (the North Star, horizons H0-H3, success metrics)
A6.  WHAT is OUT of scope?         (what HESEM deliberately does not do)
```

Part A is the "why" of HESEM. Every other Part of V9 derives from Part A.
When in doubt about whether something belongs in HESEM, the answer is
determined by Part A: does it serve the vision, the customers, and the
domains identified here?

## Part A in one paragraph

HESEM is an integrated Operations Operating Platform combining ERP, MOM,
MES, eQMS, Digital Thread, and AI advisory in one authority-disciplined
system. It is for mid-market and enterprise manufacturers (initially in
Vietnam and Asia, expanding to NA and EU) who want to consolidate the
fragmented stack of ERP + MES + eQMS + PLM + maintenance + analytics
vendors that currently costs them millions per year and creates integration
fatigue. HESEM covers 14 business domains and 95 authoritative roots. It
honors 18 international standards (ISA-95, ISA-88, 21 CFR Part 11, EU GMP
Annex 11, GAMP 5, IEC 62443, OWASP ASVS, OpenAPI, NIST AI RMF, DORA, WCAG,
ISO 27001, ISO 14971, NIST SP 800-171, FDA CSA, plus relevant industry
standards). Success means HESEM reaches release-candidate-grade across all
14 domains, with at least one vertical pack productized and at least one
pilot customer in pre-production within the planned timeline.
